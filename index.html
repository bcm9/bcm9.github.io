About Me

I am a researcher with a blend of expertise in hearing science and health technology assessment. Ultimately, my goal is to enhance health outcomes through evidence-based innovation. I'm particularly interested in:
  • Conducting clinical, operational, and economic evaluations of new medical devices and health technologies, ensuring a balanced approach to innovation.
  • Advancing the understanding and treatment of hearing impairment, with a focus on the personalisation of hearing devices to meet individual needs.
  • Leveraging technology in clinical settings to streamline processes, freeing up time for face-to-face behavioural treatments.
  • Programming to drive statistical insights, develop machine learning solutions, communicate data through visualizations, and automate tasks.
  • Machine learning diagnostics, natural language processing for qualitative analysis, and health economic models to inform decision-making.
My experience spans laboratory research, literature reviews, clinical trials, observational studies, qualitative research, and economic assessments, ensuring a holistic and balanced approach to innovation in health technology.
