About Me

I am a researcher with a blend of expertise in auditory science and health technology assessment. Ultimately, my goal is to enhance health outcomes through evidence-based innovation. I'm particularly interested in:
  • Conducting clinical, operational, and economic evaluations of new medical devices and health technologies, ensuring a balanced approach to innovation.
  • Advancing the understanding and treatment of hearing impairment, with a focus on the personalisation of hearing devices to meet individual needs.
  • Leveraging technology in clinical settings to streamline processes, freeing up time for face-to-face behavioural treatments.
  • Programming to drive insights through statistical analysis, develop solutions using machine learning, communicate data through visualizations, and automate tasks.
  • Applying machine learning for diagnostics, using natural language processing for qualitative analysis of surveys, and building health economic models to inform decision-making.
With experience ranging from the intricate details of hearing science and lab research to the broader scope of health technology, I ensure a holistic approach to innovation in my work.
